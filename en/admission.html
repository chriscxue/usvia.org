Admission Process
Step 1Parents email apply@usvia.org with the child's name, gender, age, current grade, and intended program. VIA sends an application form.
Step 2Parents and student complete the form together, scan it as a PDF, and return it to apply@usvia.org (do not send photos of the pages).
Step 3Pay the application fee.
Step 4After receiving the form and fee, VIA contacts you to schedule the student's WeChat video interview and written examination.
Step 5Interview and test results are reviewed by the admissions committee. If accepted, VIA sends an enrollment agreement to sign, scan, and return; the contract takes effect once the principal countersigns.
Step 6Pay the non-refundable tuition deposit of $5,000.
Step 7VIA issues the I-20 and acceptance letter and pays the SEVIS I-901 fee. The three documents are sent to you so the student can apply for a student visa.
Step 8If you would like help with the visa interview, VIA can recommend a travel agency (paid directly). Parents may also apply on their own.
Step 9Within 10 business days of the visa being approved, parents pay the remaining tuition and personal activity account funds.
Step 10Parents purchase flights for the designated arrival date and notify VIA to arrange airport pickup.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is VIA a school or a training center?VIA is a U.S. school accredited by a regional accreditation body designated by the U.S. Department of Education, offering middle and high school diploma education. It is not a language school, training center, or agency.
When should my child study abroad?From our years of experience, if the family's finances allow and the student is willing and reasonably self-disciplined, a child can study abroad after completing Grade 6. The younger the student, the more easily they learn and adapt — and exposure to a new culture while their worldview is forming is a lasting benefit.
Can my child attend on a tourist visa?No. Except for U.S. citizens or green card holders, students must obtain an F-1 student visa; a tourist visa cannot be used to attend VIA.
What are the school breaks?The year typically runs from late August/early September to late May/early June, with three main breaks: Thanksgiving (three days), Christmas & New Year (about two weeks; students must leave campus), and Spring Break (about one week; students must leave campus).
What should we prepare before arrival?Ensure the passport is valid; bring home-country vaccination records; treat any cavities or vision issues in advance (U.S. insurance does not cover dental or vision). Optionally, practice English typing and build reading and vocabulary before departure.
What if a student gets sick or injured?VIA arranges health insurance for every student, effective from their first day in the U.S. A nurse visits weekly, and residential staff are trained in CPR and epinephrine administration. For anything more serious, the school arranges medical care and helps obtain medication.
How large are classes?Students are grouped by ability. Class sizes never exceed 17 students, and some courses may have just one student depending on enrollment.
How does college counseling work?VIA treats the U.S. college application as a full course, guiding students through the process: understanding the application, self-analysis, researching schools through official websites, and identifying colleges that fit them — building skills they can reuse for graduate school and beyond.
